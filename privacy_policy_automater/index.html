LinkedIn Auto Poster

Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 4, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how LinkedIn Auto Poster collects, uses, stores, and protects information when users connect their LinkedIn account and use the AI content automation features.

1. Information We Collect

We may collect the following information when you use the service:

  • Account information, such as your name, email address, and user ID.
  • LinkedIn account information returned by LinkedIn OAuth, including your LinkedIn profile ID, member URN, display name, profile image, and managed organization/page identifiers when permission is granted.
  • LinkedIn OAuth access tokens and refresh tokens needed to publish approved content and retrieve account status.
  • Content data, including generated drafts, approved posts, rejected posts, topics, prompt preferences, schedules, and publishing history.
  • Analytics data, such as likes, comments, shares, impressions, clicks, follower growth, and engagement rate when available from LinkedIn.
  • Technical data, such as request logs, error logs, queue job status, IP address, browser information, and timestamps for security and reliability.

2. How We Use Information

We use collected information to:

  • Create and manage your account.
  • Connect your LinkedIn account through OAuth.
  • Generate, review, schedule, and publish LinkedIn posts.
  • Store your content preferences, prompts, and writing style memory.
  • Display content performance analytics.
  • Operate queues, scheduling, retries, and publishing workflows.
  • Improve security, prevent abuse, debug errors, and maintain service reliability.

3. LinkedIn Data and Permissions

LinkedIn Auto Poster only uses LinkedIn permissions that are necessary to connect your profile, identify your member account, identify managed organization pages where permitted, publish approved posts, and read analytics where available.

We do not sell LinkedIn profile data, OAuth tokens, post content, or analytics data. We do not publish anything to LinkedIn unless a post is approved or scheduled according to your settings.

4. Token Storage and Security

LinkedIn access tokens and refresh tokens are encrypted before storage using AES-256 encryption. Tokens are not returned by API responses and are not intentionally exposed in application logs.

We use security controls such as input validation, request logging, rate limiting, encrypted token storage, environment-based secrets, and restricted backend access to protect user data.

5. AI-Generated Content

The service may send topics, prompt instructions, writing preferences, and draft context to configured AI providers to generate or edit LinkedIn content. Do not submit confidential, sensitive, or regulated information unless you are authorized to do so.

6. Data Sharing

We may share data only with:

  • LinkedIn, when required to authenticate, publish posts, or retrieve analytics.
  • AI providers, when required to generate or edit content.
  • Infrastructure providers, such as hosting, database, queue, logging, or email services.
  • Legal or security authorities if required by law or to protect the service.

7. Data Retention

We retain account data, generated content, schedules, memory, and analytics for as long as needed to provide the service or comply with legal obligations. You may request deletion of your account data.

Disconnecting LinkedIn marks the connection as disconnected and stops future publishing through that account. Token records may be deleted or invalidated according to operational and legal requirements.

8. Your Choices

You may:

  • Disconnect your LinkedIn account from the application.
  • Delete or edit generated drafts and prompt preferences.
  • Reject generated posts before publishing.
  • Request access, correction, or deletion of your personal data.
  • Revoke the app's LinkedIn access from your LinkedIn account settings.

9. Cookies and Authentication

The service may use cookies or similar technologies for authentication, session management, CSRF protection, and application security. These cookies are necessary for the service to function.

10. International Users

Your information may be processed in countries where our hosting, infrastructure, or service providers operate. By using the service, you understand that your information may be transferred and processed outside your country of residence.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we make material changes, we will update the "Last updated" date and provide notice where appropriate.

12. Contact

For privacy questions, data requests, or account deletion requests, contact: privacy@example.com
